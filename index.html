How to use this tracker
1
Refresh prices

Click "↻ Refresh Prices" top right anytime. Pulls latest market price from Yahoo Finance. Works for stocks, ETFs, and crypto tickers. Not live — snapshot on demand.

2
Add a new position

Click "+ Add position" → enter ticker, name, number of shares, your average cost, account (TFSA/RRSP/Crypto), and signal. Hit Add. It saves in your browser automatically.

3
Reading the signals

BUY = keep accumulating regularly. HOLD = don't sell, don't rush to buy more. WATCH = review it, leaning toward exit. EXIT = sell and redeploy into better positions.

4
Day % column

Shows today's price change vs yesterday's close. Green = up today, Red = down. This is how you'd catch NVDA being up 4.5% or down 6% — just hit refresh.

5
All-time return

Calculated from your avg cost vs current price. Green = profit, Red = loss. This is your real return, not shown in Wealthsimple's confusing way.

6
Dividends tab

Shows all your dividend-paying positions with estimated annual income. BMO, CNQ, SU, VFV, VDY all show here. Great for tracking passive income growth.

7
Watchlist tab

Shows recommended ETFs/stocks you don't own yet (XEQT, VDY, XIU etc.) with live prices so you can decide when to buy. Update this as your strategy evolves.

8
Data saves locally

Your portfolio data saves in your browser (localStorage). It persists between sessions on the same device/browser. If you clear browser data it resets to defaults.

9
Crypto tickers

For crypto use: ETH-USD, SOL-USD, BTC-USD format when adding. Regular stocks use normal tickers: NVDA, AAPL, BMO.TO for TSX stocks.

10
TSX stocks

Canadian stocks need .TO suffix for prices: BMO.TO, SU.TO, CNQ.TO, VFV.TO, VDY.TO. US stocks use plain ticker: NVDA, AAPL, PLTR.
